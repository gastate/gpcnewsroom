Program offers gateway to college for high school dropouts

by Kysa Daniels

As a teenager, Sheneka Kirlew transferred in and out of so many high schools she eventually lost count.

“I wasn’t doing what I was supposed to do,” the 20-year-old recalls. She remembers realizing that she wouldn’t graduate from high school as planned. Then, two years ago—at age 18—Kirlew learned about a unique collaboration between Georgia Perimeter College and DeKalb County Schools called Gateway to College Academy.

Gateway targets high school dropouts and students like Kirlew who aren’t thriving in traditional high schools and offers them a chance to attain a high school diploma and accrue college credit at the same time.

This past December, an energetic, yet tearful Kirlew and five of her Gateway classmates donned caps and gowns to participate in their high school graduation—and celebrate their simultaneous success as college students.

“When I enrolled at Gateway, I took matters into my own hands and started to be more responsible for my actions,” Kirlew said in her graduation remarks. “I have grown in maturity, motivation and perseverance.”

Noah Jackson, the only male in the graduating group, says he earned 16 semester hours while in Gateway. He’ll continue at Georgia Perimeter for another semester before heading to Savannah State University as a biology major. Gateway, Jackson says, has prepared him well.

“I used to procrastinate with everything, but soon after taking those college courses, I realized and changed my ways,” Jackson said. “Now I set up weekly checkpoints and make sure I stay on top of all of my assignments.”

Iman Ali credits Gateway with her academic and personal development as well. “The freedom of not having assistant principals at the end of each hall telling me to run to class before getting locked out was such a relief,” Ali recalled during her graduation reflection. “I also realized that I couldn’t let the freedom get to me either, because I had to remember: with freedom comes responsibility.”

With 25 college credits already, 18-year-old Ali is well on her way toward her goal of becoming a pediatric nurse and eventual pediatrician.

Currently, there are 95 students in the Gateway program, with another 10 scheduled to graduate this spring.

March 3, 2014
