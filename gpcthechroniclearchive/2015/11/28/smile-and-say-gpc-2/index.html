Smile and Say “GPC!”

Home » Features » Smile and Say “GPC!”

by Rebecca Rakoczy

 

The morning air hung hot, heavy and humid, even for July in the South—and it wasn’t 9:30 a.m. yet. More than 100 faculty, staff, students, retirees and alumni of the college—dressed in colorful attire—made their way across the damp grass of the Clarkston Campus soccer field.

 

The mission was to create human letters spelling out “GPC” for a special feature in The Chronicle magazine, a vivid photo tribute celebrating the special qualities, history and memories of the college.

 

Creating the look of the photo required intense behind-the-scenes logistics. The Fine Arts technical crew, led by Jennifer Jenkins and Lizz Emerson-Dorsey, spearheaded the coordination between the Office of Marketing and Communications, photo participants, Plant Operations and others, adding their experienced hand in organizing the event.  Jenkins and Emerson-Dorsey worked with photographer Bill Roa and art director Suzie Amsberry—visiting the site weeks before, taking scene-setting photos, plotting them on the computer and then pacing out and marking outlines of the letters on the ground the day before. Jenkins made sure there would be a bit of shade and plenty of water to keep the crowd hydrated. It was needed. The heat was sweltering.

 

Some participants arrived wearing outfits that signified their respective roles or academic disciplines at the college. Some wore hats, masks or Georgia Perimeter College spirit wear. Others carried props. All came to celebrate Georgia Perimeter.
The enthusiastic crowd sweated, smiled and looked up—and up—at the photographer, art director and videographer, who hung high above in a bucket crane. With a grand gesture toward the camera above, the group yelled “GPC.” And a moment was captured, in all its sweaty glory.

 

Thanks to all who participated. If you were there, see if you can find yourself in the Cover photo (and in the list below)!
